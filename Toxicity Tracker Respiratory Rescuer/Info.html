Good

  • Medical nebulizers - I am used for controlled inhalation of medications in respiratory therapy.
  • Air fresheners - I release chemicals that are typically present in very low concentrations.
  • Saline solution - I mimic the body's natural fluids, providing a gentle and non-irritating environment.
  • Steam from boiling water - I am composed of water vapor, which, when inhaled, provides moisture to the respiratory tract.
  • Perfumes - I have volatile components that are designed to evaporate quickly, minimizing prolonged exposure.
  • Candles - My combustion process produces minimal harmful byproducts.
  • Wood smoke - Matters and irritants in me are present in minimal concentrations.
  • Printer emissions - Inhaling trace amounts of emissions from me in typical use is generally safe.
  • Dust - My particles are typically larger and heavier and are likely to be trapped in the upper respiratory tract.
  • Isopropyl alcohol - I evaporate quickly, reducing the likelihood of prolonged inhalation.

Bad

  • Paint Thinners - I can shorten how you breathe and can make you dizzy.
  • Deodorant - I can make you cough and wheeze.
  • Bleach - I can irritate your eyes and throat.
  • Gasoline - I can cause headaches.
  • Lighter fluid - I contain butane and can cause nausea.
  • Cigarette Smoke - I can give you a chronic cough.
  • Pesticides - I can cause irritation and inflammation in the airways, leading to difficulty in breathing.
  • Chlorine - I can form hydrochloric acid that will irritate and damage the respiratory tissues.
  • Marker fumes - I have volatile organic compounds which, when inhaled, can cause coughing.
  • Acetone - I can inflame the mucous membranes in the nose and throat.
